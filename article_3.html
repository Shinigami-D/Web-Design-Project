Ghosts outlast Thunderhawks in 135-116 Shootout, Improve to 3-0

Peter Parker | October 26th, 2024

The Ghosts continued their hot streak, securing their third straight win with a decisive 135-116 victory on the road against the Thunderhawks. Fueled by dominant performances from their stars and key contributions off the bench, the Ghosts overwhelmed their opponents with efficient shooting and relentless scoring pressure.

Offensive Masterclass

The Ghosts put on an offensive clinic, shooting 53.4% from the field and an impressive 46.2% from three-point range. While the Thunderhawks kept pace early, the Ghosts’ explosive third quarter, where they outscored the home team 38-28, created a gap that the Thunderhawks couldn’t close.

Key Performers

  • Damien Cross (#45): Cross was unstoppable, dropping a season-high 30 points on 11-of-18 shooting, including 6-of-11 from three. His offensive versatility gave the Thunderhawks fits all night.
  • Xavier Holt (#31): Holt was a perfect 10-for-10 from the field, showcasing his efficiency and finishing ability en route to 23 points. He added five rebounds and two blocks, reinforcing his two-way dominance.
  • Marcus King (#10): King delivered his best game of the season, scoring 23 points on a scorching 9-of-11 shooting. His four three-pointers kept the Thunderhawks’ defense scrambling.

Supporting Cast Shines

While the starters carried the scoring load, the bench made critical contributions:

  • Quentin Reeves (#3): Reeves continued his efficient shooting, scoring 10 points and hitting two clutch three-pointers in the second half.
  • Cameron Vance (#2): Vance added 14 points, including a momentum-shifting three-pointer in the third quarter that extended the Ghosts' lead to double digits.
  • Nolan Brooks (#5): Brooks found his rhythm, scoring 11 points on 3-of-7 shooting from beyond the arc.

Key Impact Moments

  • Damien Cross’ Deep Bombs: Cross buried three three-pointers in the third quarter, including a dagger from 30 feet that silenced the Thunderhawks' crowd.
  • Xavier Holt’s Perfect Night: Xavier Holt’s Perfect Night: Holt’s flawless shooting included a thunderous dunk midway through the fourth quarter, capping off an 8-0 run that put the game out of reach.
  • Defensive Stands: The Ghosts recorded 10 blocks and 13 steals, with Jordan Hayes (#4) contributing two blocks and 13 rebounds to lock down the defensive boards.

Trends Emerging

  • Damien Cross’ Ascendancy: With back-to-back 19-point and 30-point performances, Cross is proving himself as the Ghosts’ go-to scorer in high-stakes moments.
  • Elite Team Shooting: The Ghosts have consistently shot above 50% from the field in all three games, making them one of the most efficient offensive units in the league.
  • Xavier Holt’s Reliability: Holt’s combination of rebounding, shot-blocking, and interior scoring makes him a cornerstone for both offense and defense.

What’s Next?

The Ghosts now sit at 3-0, with their balanced offense and defensive intensity making them an early favorite in the league. Next, they travel to face the Titans, a team known for its scrappy defense. Can the Ghosts maintain their scoring brilliance and continue their perfect start?
