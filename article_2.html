Ghosts Hold Off Falcons for a 113-101 Victory in Home Opener

Peter Parker | October 25th, 2024

The Ghosts brought the energy to their home opener, defeating the Falcons 113-101 in a game defined by balanced scoring and key defensive plays. After a high-scoring first quarter, the Ghosts found their rhythm in the second and never looked back, improving to 2-0 on the season.

Second Quarter Breakaway

Trailing by one point after the first quarter, the Ghosts came alive in the second, outscoring the Falcons 37-26. Logan Pierce (#32) was lights-out from beyond the arc, nailing five three-pointers en route to a season-high 19 points. His sharp shooting shifted momentum and gave the Ghosts a comfortable halftime lead.

The defense also tightened, with Jordan Hayes (#4) picking up two steals in the quarter and Xavier Holt (#31) grabbing critical rebounds to deny the Falcons second-chance opportunities. Holt's 11 total boards highlighted his dominance in the paint.

Key Performers

  • Logan Pierce (#32): Pierce had a breakout performance, shooting 7-for-9 from the field and an impressive 5-for-7 from three. His timely shooting stretched the Falcons' defense and kept the Ghosts ahead.
  • Damien Cross (#45): Although Cross struggled with efficiency (8-for-21 from the field), his relentless scoring drive kept the Ghosts' offense flowing. He matched Pierce’s 19 points while adding five assists.
  • Xavier Holt (#31): Holt recorded his first double-double of the season with 17 points and 11 rebounds, continuing his dominance on the boards and defensive end.

Bench Struggles but Vance Delivers

The Ghosts’ bench cooled off compared to Game 1, contributing 29 points compared to 64 in the opener. Cameron Vance (#2) remained a steady presence, adding 13 points and four assists while hitting 67% of his shots.

Key Impact Moments

  • Logan Pierce’s 3-Point Barrage: Pierce drained back-to-back triples midway through the second quarter, sparking a 12-2 run that gave the Ghosts a double-digit lead.
  • Cameron Vance’s Fourth-Quarter Dime: With the Falcons mounting a late push, Vance threaded a perfect bounce pass to Quentin Reeves (#3) for an easy bucket, quelling their momentum.
  • Jordan Hayes’ Defensive Presence: Hayes picked up four steals in total, with a crucial third-quarter takeaway leading to a fast-break layup that extended the lead.

Trends Emerging

  • Balanced Scoring: All five starters scored in double digits, a testament to the Ghosts’ offensive balance. Pierce's emergence adds another scoring threat to complement Hayes, Cross, and Holt.
  • Strong Starts from the Starters: The Ghosts’ starting five combined for 84 points, a dominant showing that carried them through some bench inconsistency.
  • Defensive Intensity: The Ghosts had 13 steals, a clear sign that their defensive identity is taking shape.

Looking Ahead

With two wins under their belt, the Ghosts have proven they can win in different ways—by relying on their bench in Game 1 and leaning on their starters in Game 2. However, consistency from the bench and efficiency from key players like Damien Cross will be areas to watch moving forward.

The Ghosts will hit the road again for their next game against the Thunderhawks, hoping to build on their hot start and maintain their unbeaten record.
